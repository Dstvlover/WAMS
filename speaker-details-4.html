Guest Speaker

Prof Kwasi Appeaning Addo

    • Prof. Kwasi Appeaning Addo is a coastal processes specialist and the director for the Institute for Environment and Sanitation Studies, University of Ghana. His research interest include shoreline morphodynamics; coastal erosion and flooding studies; coastal vulnerability assessment under sea level change scenarios; application of drone and video technology in nearshore coastal studies; coastal zone management and delta systems studies. He is presently part of a team researching into transformation adaptation and equitable resilience of Sargassum in West Africa and the Caribbean. Kwasi has consulted for several organization in the area of coastal erosion and flooding management. He is a member of the Expert Group of the High Level Panel for Sustainable Ocean Economy involving 13 Heads of Governments, technical advisor to the West African coast observation mission (WACOM) project involving 11 West African countries, and a member of the International Pool of Experts, UN Ocean Affairs and the Law of the Sea. He is also a member of several professional bodies and a visiting scholar to universities. Kwasi has authored books and book chapters, and several peer-reviewed journal publications to his credit.
