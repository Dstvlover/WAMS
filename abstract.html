Abstract Submission

Instructions for applicants:

Please submit an abstract no more than 500 words. Applicants may choose only ONE session theme to apply under and may submit only ONE abstract. Clearly indicate which thematic area you are submitting your abstract to. Eligible applications must include the following information:

•Name

•Current Institution

•Contact Information

•The session theme for which you would like to submit your abstract for consideration

•Abstract, 500 words or less

Abstracts will be reviewed as they are submitted; the deadline to submit an abstract for consideration is 31st May 2023. You will receive an email by 15 June 2023 if your abstract is chosen for the symposium. Eligible abstracts will focus on work being led along the west coast of Africa. Strong abstracts for consideration will include:

•An example(s) of a project(s) led or co-led by the applicant that directly addresses the session theme identified for consideration, and justifies the context of the project example in relation to the theme;

•Background information that builds on previous research or events, underlining the importance of the work and the potential for impact in the discipline(s), local communities, and/or region writ large;

•Specific project goals or outcomes that have been or will be achieved, and a short discussion highlighting any successes in the work so far, and barriers that have been considered and/or overcome in the span of the project implementation; and

•Details on project timelines, locations, and stakeholders that provide necessary background and context on the work being highlighted.

Above, “Project” can mean an example of a case study or research, conservation, or management effort in support of the session theme. We strongly encourage and will prioritize abstracts submitted by applicants based in and/or from West Africa. Support towards travel and accommodation may be provided to some in-region applicants who are invited to speak at the West Africa Marine Science Symposium. However, we encourage applicants who can cover their travel costs to do so.
